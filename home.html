01 — The Problem

Every small business in India loses money to slow hours

P.01

Predictable Dead Zones

Mid-morning, post-lunch, late afternoon — the same hours bleed revenue every single day. No affordable real-time tool exists to convert that idle capacity.

P.02

Perishable Inventory Loss

Unsold food, fresh produce, daily stock — when it doesn't move, it's a direct loss. Vendors need a fast, low-friction channel to clear inventory before close.

P.03

No Affordable Local Marketing

Social ads reach the wrong geography. Delivery platforms charge 15–30% commission. The walk-in customer — who costs nothing to serve — has no discovery layer.

04 — Why Now

Four structural forces converging at once

Enabling Force 01

UPI at Massive Scale

India's UPI infrastructure enables frictionless in-store payments with near-zero cost. The payment layer that makes instant offer redemption seamless is already in every Indian's pocket.

Enabling Force 02

Smartphone Penetration in Urban India

Mobile-first behaviour is now default across urban SMB owners and their customers. A proximity-based discovery platform is viable at scale in ways it wasn't three years ago.

Enabling Force 03

Delivery Platform Fatigue

Rising commissions and dependency on Swiggy/Zomato are pushing vendors to seek direct customer relationships. Zteeel is positioned as the affordable, commission-free alternative for walk-in revenue.

Enabling Force 04

No Direct Competitor in This Space

Food aggregators serve delivery. Coupon platforms serve static deals. Social ads serve awareness. Nobody is building real-time, proximity-first, walk-in revenue tooling for offline SMBs.

06 — Roadmap

Clear milestones, measurable outcomes

Phase 01

Launch Pilot — 100 Vendors

Deploy in 2 high-density neighbourhoods. Measure redemption rate, average order value lift, and vendor retention at 30 and 60 days.

Phase 02

Validate Product-Market Fit

Achieve >40% redemption rate on offers and >15% AOV lift for active vendors. Use data to refine proximity radius and offer timing engine.

Phase 03

Scale to 1,000 Vendors

Expand to 3–5 cities using cluster onboarding with local vendor associations. Activate referral program. Begin building the data moat.

Phase 04

10,000 Vendors — Series A Ready

Proven unit economics across multiple cities. Predictable MRR, low churn, and a proprietary demand dataset with defensible competitive moat.
